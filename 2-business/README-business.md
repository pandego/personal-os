# Business Domain

Business work that should stay separate from public content and private personal tracking.

## Purpose
Use `2-business/` for the work that supports consulting, delivery, positioning, and client operations.

This domain is for things like:
- portfolio assets and case-study support
- outbound and lead-generation workflows
- active client delivery spaces
- reusable business materials that should not live in content or personal folders

## Structure
- `01-portfolio/` - portfolio drafts, finished assets, code samples, and swipe files
- `02-outbound/` - newsletters, LinkedIn outreach, and Upwork-specific outreach systems
- `03-clients/` - per-client workspaces created from the `_template/`

## Folder Guidance
### `01-portfolio/`
Use this for the assets that explain what you do and prove capability.

Suggested usage:
- `01-drafts/` - rough portfolio copy, drafts of project pages, positioning experiments
- `02-done/` - approved portfolio copy or finalized business-facing assets
- `03-code/` - small code examples, snippets, or supporting implementation artifacts
- `04-swipe-files/` - examples of strong portfolios, case studies, and offer pages

### `02-outbound/`
Use this for proactive business development.

Keep it organized by channel:
- `01-newsletters/` - newsletter ideas, campaign drafts, and publishing support
- `02-linkedin-outreach/` - connection messages, follow-ups, and outreach experiments
- `03-upwork/` - proposal drafts, reusable answer patterns, job-fit notes

### `03-clients/`
Use this for delivery work that belongs to specific clients.

Start from `_template/` when opening a new client workspace so every client folder has a predictable shape.

## Workflow Principles
- Keep public-facing content in `3-content/`, not here.
- Keep private journaling, reviews, and knowledge capture in `1-personal/`, not here.
- Prefer reusable assets and templates over scattered one-off notes.
- Client-sensitive material should stay inside the correct client folder.
- Portfolio proof should be concise, concrete, and easy to reuse later.

## Commands / Skills That Matter Here
| Command / Skill | Use |
|-----------------|-----|
| `/get-started` | shape the system around your needs if the workspace is still fresh |
| `/review` | reflect on business progress during weekly or yearly reviews |

## Definition of Done
A business artifact is in good shape when:
- it is in the correct business subfolder
- its audience is obvious
- it is either clearly draft or clearly finished
- it can be reused later without guessing context
